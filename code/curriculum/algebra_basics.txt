# Algebra Basics - Curriculum Notes

Topic: Introduction to Linear Equations

Key Concepts:
1. A linear equation represents a straight line when graphed on a coordinate plane.
2. The standard form of a linear equation is Ax + By = C.
3. The slope-intercept form is y = mx + b, where:
   - m = slope of the line
   - b = y-intercept (where the line crosses the y-axis)
4. To solve for y, isolate the variable using inverse operations.

Example Problem:
Solve for y: 3x + 2y = 10

Step 1: Subtract 3x from both sides → 2y = -3x + 10
Step 2: Divide both sides by 2 → y = -1.5x + 5

Practice Questions:
1. Write 2x + 5y = 10 in slope-intercept form.
2. What is the slope of y = 4x - 2?
3. Determine the y-intercept of y = -3x + 7.

Learning Goal:
Students should understand how to identify slope and intercept, and how to graph a linear equation.
